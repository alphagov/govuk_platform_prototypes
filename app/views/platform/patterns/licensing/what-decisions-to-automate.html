Automating decisions

Automating decisions to give permission, makes services faster for users and cheaper for government.

Computers are better at some types of decisions, humans are better at others.

Automatic decisions

Automated decisions are yes-no questions answered by software using data.

For example:

  • Are you over 18?
  • Do you have a criminal record?
  • Do you have right to work in the UK?
  • Do you have a Limited Company?
  • Do you live in an area of Significant Scientific interest

Software and data is often better at granting or refusing licence, when the only condition of a user getting or not getting permission is based just yes-no questions.

Examples of automatic licences

If you’re not sure about your nationality, try to find out from an official document like a passport or national ID card.

We need to know your nationality so we can work out which elections you’re entitled to vote in. If you can’t provide your nationality, you’ll have to send copies of identity documents through the post.

Human decisions

Human decisions are expert judgements about a users suitability for a licence, informed by data, offline activites or rules which often change.

For example:

  • Is this user able to drive safely?
  • Is this safe place to store firearms?
  • Does this rare health condition effect driving?
  • Is this user eligible for visa?
