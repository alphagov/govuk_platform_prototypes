In person decisions

Some human decisions about a users suitability for a licence are only possible offline.

Activities like driving tests, home visits and site inspections need someone to be there in person. Being in-person makes it possible to make a expert judgement, on which granting a licence relies.

Although this type of activites are done locally, due to their physical nature, they should still be consistent to use and deliver.

Mapping a service is not a rule

A map is just a way to organise information a team has discovered about it's users and service.

Only having performed a Discovery, should a team consider mapping how it's service currently works.

The activity of mapping a service can become a distraction from learning about your user and service. Only map the current service when:

  • your users' needs are known
  • your service's needs are known
  • deciding what and how to measure service performance
  • you need data to show the impact of potential design decisions
  • a single
  • you are ready to share your understanding of the current service, before exploring how to design it.
